Part 5: Cloth Sim
Explain in your own words what is a shader program and how vertex and fragment shaders work together to create lighting and material effects.

A shader program is user-defined code that a GPU uses to execute parts of the graphics pipeline. The (basic) pipeline of a shader program consists of two sequential parts: (1) the vertex shader, and (2) the fragment shader. Vertex shaders are responsible for the geometry of vertices in the rendered model. The fragment shader operates on the output of the vertex shader, using "fragments" of the rasterized model. In particular, it computes the color of rasterized fragments using the inputted vertex information and some specified shading method.

Explain the Blinn-Phong shading model in your own words. Show a screenshot of your Blinn-Phong shader outputting only the ambient component, a screen shot only outputting the diffuse component, a screen shot only outputting the specular component, and one using the entire Blinn-Phong model.

The Blinn-Phong shading model is a 3-part per-vertex shading model that computes the light intensity incident according to the following equation:

where \(k_a, k_d, k_s\) are the ambient, diffuse, and specular coefficients; \(I, \ell, r = \|\ell\|\) are the intensity, ray displacement, and distance to the light source; \(n\) is the surface normal at the vertex; \(I_a\) is the ambient light intensity; \(h\) is the half-vector (i.e. bisector) of \(\ell, n\); and \(p\) is the specular power.

The first term represents ambient light, or light that would hypothetically arrive after more than one bounce. The second term represents diffuse light, which is a uniform hemispherical reflection. The final (and third) term represents specular light, approximating light reflected off of the scene in a mirror-like way.

For our shading, we used the following constant values: \(k_a = 0.2, k_d = 1, k_s = 0.8, I_a = 0.8, p = 20\).

Both bump mapping and displacement mapping display the texture on the cloth's surface. However, bump mapping doesn't change the texture of cloth, so the resulting shading looks a bit "flat" and unrealistic. On the other hand, displacement mapping modifies the cloth vertices according to their texture height, which makes the texture shading more realistic.
